Bison is all about offense and stealing turns. He's got a lot of great midrange normals that either have superior reach like 5MK or are built around stealing or keeping his turn like 5HP. He has several gimmicks like his charge leap special, Shadow Rise, that gives him access to the infamous Devil's Reverse and Head Press, and a new Backfist Combo that plants a time-delayed psycho power bomb on the opponent that gives him extended pressure potential.

## What is he good at?

He's got strong presence in the midrange and up close. His mediums are all very good at establishing his presence on screen, his 5HP is an oppressive conditioning tool and counterpoke, and since scissor kick is now on quarter-circle forward it can be used as a spacing trap and pressure tool.

Landing scissor kick in general puts you in a decent situation: both light and medium put you in a strike/throw scenario where you can do meaty throw or button, making every scissor kick you land a threat. In a game like SF6 where a lot of characters leverage 2MK drive rush cancels, Bison can also throw out scissor kick and fish for low crushes.

His corner pressure is oppressive and straightforward. On top of having a fast drive rush that covers good distance and a throw loop, his psycho mine from Backfist Combo allows him to either force the opponent to make a hasty decision or chip away at the opponent's drive gauge. He's often safe or plus in these scenarios and can abuse his grounded target combos

Additionally, his damage output and drive efficiency is exceptional, often only having to spend 2 to 3 drive bars for the best of all worlds: damage, corner carry, okizeme.

## Where is he weak?

Bison is a very SF6-coded character in that he cannot be played passively for a prolonged period of time and should be throwing out yeet specials and turning green. As a Bison player, you have to start the conversation: poking with 5MK, pressuring with scissor kick, preempting your opponent's offense with 2MP and 5HP, etc. The instant you let up, you have very little recourse against your opponent's own offense and pressure and you become predictable.

Bison's anti-air game is also somewhat lacking. It's serviceable and simply landing it in lower ranks will put you miles ahead of your opponent, but he's vulnerable to characters with tricky air maneuverability like Cammy and Jamie. His grounded anti-air, 2HP, is especially vulnerable to characters with good crossups. His air-to-air target combo is good but has to be converted into a special for it to be anything other than defensive.

Against equal or better opponents who have practiced the matchup, you have to be on your a-game because they'll have labbed punishing scissor kick and either countering or evading all of your Shadow Rise options. Your pressure has to become smarter and more reliant on psycho mine.
