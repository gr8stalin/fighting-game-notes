The original gangsta. A very straightforward character who can play the drive system pretty well, his simplicity and general safety after buttons coupled with high damage make him a very formidable character.

In Street Fighter 6, his new addition is "Denjin" which allows him to channel his ki as an install, enhancing his fireball, super 1, and Hashogeki.

## What is he good at?

Ryu is just very good in general. 

- His anti-airs are consistent and provide good oki
- His specials have oki that allow him to loop the same sequence over and over again to devastating results
- His damage is very high per interaction for how low the execution requirements are
- His lights allow him to control neutral extremely well and access his oki instantly on conversion
- He can be belligerent with normally unsafe buttons (5HP) thanks to his 214P special, Hashogeki
- Throw loop in the corner (throw > whiff jab)
- Great fireball game coupled with Denjin install

## Where is he weak?

- His movement speed isn't the best, relying on button pressure vs movement for spacing traps
- No air throw meaning a lot of situations you'd airthrow as Chun, Juri, etc. are off the table and you have to rely on jumpback jab, which gives up precious offensive space. He does have a good a2a off j.mp
- His drive rush distance and speed is pretty average but serviceable; not good (i.e., Juri) but not bad (i.e., Chun)
- His pressure is very strong but linear: no fun and powerful tools like Chun instant air legs to mess with people
- Odd idiosyncrasies in his combo structure, i.e., jumpin heavy > medium > medium doesn't exist anymore from SF5, have to do jumpin heavy > heavy or jumpin heavy > medium > light
