## Midscreen

Marisa has nothing but superman dive, jump, and slime rush at 2+ grid squares (duh). To avoid getting burned out midscreen by jumpscare superman dive/gladius, just parry on reaction. You can get a perfect parry but you'll always keep your drive gauge if you don't.

You can contest superman dive with jab but you'll more than likely trade with it, so parry is a better option if they're jumpscare superman dives.

They tend to look for gladius at 1.5 grid squares. [Can lk legs break gladius?]

Winning neutral isn't enough because she can do 70% off one touch, you need to keep up your own offense and do walk forward/microwalk 2mk. That beats both regular and ex gladius startup. Gladius tends to "accidentally" win against poke normals and Marisa will always convert better than you will, especially off of a whiff punish. This is why it's important to walk her down with 2mk and let the Marisa know that Gladius will be off the table.

Bane mentions that wakeup (ex) gladius is an option he'll consider if he knows that if it lands, it's doing massive damage.

When trying to whiff punish gladius, it's best to find a poke that's reasonably safe and can cancel into drive rush. Jab is actually not a terrible option. The whiff punishes have to be labbed per character and we have to lab the reaction to gladius and charged gladius. Sweep is not the move against good Marisa players.

Marisa risk/reward is extremely skewed because just putting shit out there as jumpscares and aggression is extremely rewarding and feeds into her win condition in all cases.

When she does drive rush 2mp on block, you can't walk back because 2lk > 2lp 214p will hit you and she'll get the whole-ass drive meter she spent back. She can also go right into command grab after the dr 2mp.

If Marisa does drive rush 5hp, she's only +1. You can try something cheeky to take your turn back if she's intent on pressing her advantage.

## Offense

If you find yourself in that .5 grid square range where it'll turn into a battle of lights, you can try for a crossup jump. Her a2a is really good and most of her offense and defense is centralized on literally one button (medium punch: 2mp, 5mp tc, j.mp a2a tc), but you should try to contest her here if you've been showing that you'll contest her close.

## Defense

When you get knocked down by Marisa and she sends the mp target combo into gladius, you have to parry in between. If you land a perfect parry you can obviously turn the tide, but parrying alone will save your drive meter.

If you start parrying, the Marisa player is forced to introduce mp target combo -> fake throw and the target combo is -7. If the Marisa starts doing this or cancelling into fake drive rush, you can ex dp in that spot. If they drc 2lk 2lp the dp won't come out.

If the Marisa is burnt out while doing the mp tc, dial in ex dp (or super 1 if it's fast enough) because there's nothing the Marisa can do there to avoid a punish.

Charged 4hp on block in the corner is a 4-way mixup and if you block the whole blockstring you lose almost 2 drive bars. If you get here, you're genuinely guessing for your life: the sequence is invulnerable to drev and DI, blocking means you lose drive gauge, Marisa can turn it into a grab sequence, you cannot jump the sequence. One thing you can do  is try to parry. This situation is basically checkmate unless you guess.

You can parry between 2lk > 2lp and get a perfect parry off the 2lp. However, if you block the whole thing, she's out of range to be thrown but in range of her other buttons.

If she does 6mp into stance-throw, watch the range because you can throw her if the 6mp puts her close enough. She cannot walk out of it.

## Side-notes

Parrying after a plus button if you think they're going to run offense is a decent way to break up someone's offense. It forces them to include button > walkup throw into their kit, meaning you can press after their plus button if you see the shift happen.

Bane likes to throw people a lot during the first round: it helps solidify whether or not they're going to try to parry (big damage from throw), they're going to take the throw (regular throw damage adds up), or if they're going to mash (if you do meaty throw it shows up as counter hit).

Doing blockstrings and stuff like that takes away drive gauge. Even though your opponent may be blocking moves, they're taking drive gauge chip if they're not parrying. If they're parrying, you can throw them for 30%.
