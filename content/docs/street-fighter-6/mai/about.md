Mai is another fireball drive rush character with throw loops. Her gimmick revolves around uncharged/charged fireballs and her Super 1 gives her 5 "flame stocks" that enhance her specials and damage, and it's possible to loop your combos into Super 1, replenishing your stocks after usage. I initially wrote this in OneNote before people started figuring out how Top 1 she is, so this description sounds whatever but she has basically become the demon of Street Fighter 6.

## What is she good at?

When I first wrote this people hadn't really cracked the Mai code.

Her fireball drive rush offense is obnoxious even among characters with similar fireball drive rush functionality. One ex fireball is enough to give you drive rush into strike/throw/overhead, and if the opponent does hold parry to circumvent the drive chip, you can drive rush for a punish counter throw.

Situations like this end up being drive meter positive with Mai because you can set up another fan or get a button with her, chipping the opponent's meter while preserving/recovering yours.

She has exceptional pokes and her lights can be frustrating since they're not as minus as most lights. Her pokes feed into 236K cancels, which have insane corner carry much like Ken tatsu. 

Mai is also slightly less reliant on the drive rush cancel aspect of the game for conversions. Converting 5MP, 2MP, or 2MK into 236KK sets up divekick oki and converting into 214PP allows for midscreen linking into Super 1, feeding into her win condition for less drive meter than a drc.

Everyone is good when they have someone cornered but Mai's special flavor of it is looping charged fans into strike/throw/shimmy. One of the dastardly things about her corner pressure is that after a throw, she can dash up to threaten a throw loop but backdash instead and be at perfect spacing for a shimmy since it still looks like she's in range for throw.

## Where is she weak?

Again, when I first wrote this people hadn't really cracked the Mai code. Now that she's been figured out, she genuinely doesn't have major weaknesses, but has a few minor ones that make playing against her at least possible.

Mai's damage without flame stocks is slightly lower than most of the cast. Ryu and Chun will reach about 32% damage on midscreen dp punishes, Mai will do 29% optimally. Mai's damage without flame stocks normalizes once the opponent is cornered.

Mai's blockstrings aren't that great as her mediums are around -3 to -4 on block. Her continual pressure typically ends once 2MP is blocked.

Mai's 2MK is sorta stubby given how far buttons can reach in this game. It's still an effective button but you will be surprised when it doesn't reach.

Uncharged fans (fireballs) are important for clipping your opponent but since they can be destroyed by pretty much anything else in the game, she loses any fireball war by default when using uncharged fans, and she isn't as effective at stopping characters like Blanka and Honda vs other fireball equipped characters. This *usually* doesn't come into play now that she's been figured out because most Mai players are going straight into OD fan.
